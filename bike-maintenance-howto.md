# Bike Maintenance HOW-TO

## Introduction
This guide is designed to help you understand and resolve typical bike maintenance issues, with a focus on the VF750 model. Proper and regular maintenance ensures safety, longevity, and the best performance from your bike.

## Typical Issues
1. **Engine Troubles**
   - Check and replace spark plugs as needed.
   - Ensure the carburetor is clean and adjusted.
2. **Oil Leaks**
   - Inspect gaskets and seals for any wear or damage.
   - Regularly check and top up engine oil levels.
3. **Battery Issues**
   - Check battery voltage periodically.
   - Clean terminals and ensure proper connections.
4. **Braking System**
   - Inspect brake pads for wear and replace as needed.
   - Check brake fluid levels and ensure there are no leaks.

## Standard Operations
1. **Oil Change**
   - Change the oil every 3,000-5,000 km or as recommended in the user manual.
   - Use high-quality motorcycle engine oil.
2. **Tire Maintenance**
   - Check tire pressure regularly.
   - Inspect for wear and replace if the tread depth is low.
3. **Chain Maintenance**
   - Lubricate the chain every 500-700 km.
   - Adjust chain tension to the recommended slack.
4. **Cleaning and Lubrication**
   - Clean your bike regularly to prevent dirt and grime build-up.
   - Lubricate all moving parts to ensure smooth operation.

## Safety Notes
- Always wear protective gloves and eyewear during maintenance.
- Use proper tools to avoid damaging bike components.
- Work in a well-ventilated area, especially when dealing with fuel or oil.

## Tips and Resources
- Refer to the VF750 user manual for detailed specifications and procedures.
- Join online forums or communities for VF750 enthusiasts for shared tips and experiences.
- Keep a log of your maintenance tasks to track when procedures were last performed.
