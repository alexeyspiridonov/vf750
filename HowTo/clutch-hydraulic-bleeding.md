# Clutch hydraulic bleeding

Use DOT 4 fluid and protect painted parts before opening the reservoir. The clutch hydraulic system uses a handlebar master cylinder and a slave cylinder with a bleed valve on the engine side.

## Prep

- Put the bike upright on level ground.
- Turn the bars so the clutch master cylinder reservoir sits level.
- Cover the tank and fairing panels with rags because brake fluid damages paint.
- Clean dirt from the reservoir cap and from around the slave cylinder bleed valve.
- Fit a clear hose to the slave cylinder bleed valve and run the hose into a catch bottle.

## Bleeding Procedure

1. Remove the reservoir cap and diaphragm.
2. Fill the reservoir with fresh DOT 4 fluid from a sealed container.
3. Pump the clutch lever three or four times and hold it against the bar.
4. Crack the slave cylinder bleed valve just long enough for fluid and air to move into the hose.
5. Close the bleed valve before releasing the lever.
6. Refill the reservoir as needed and never let it run dry.
7. Repeat until the hose shows clean fluid with no bubbles and the lever firms up.

## If The Lever Stays Spongy

- Tap the hose, master cylinder, and slave cylinder lightly to free trapped bubbles.
- Leave the lever tied back to the bar for a few hours or overnight, then bleed again.
- Reverse-bleed from the slave cylinder upward with a syringe if the system was fully emptied or rebuilt.
- Check banjo bolts, crush washers, and the bleed valve for seepage.
- If pressure still will not build, inspect the master cylinder and slave cylinder seals.

## Final Checks

- Fluid level should finish above the lower mark in the reservoir.
- Install the diaphragm and cap before the final lever test.
- The lever should feel firm and the clutch should disengage cleanly when selecting first gear.
- Rinse off any spilled fluid immediately with water.
