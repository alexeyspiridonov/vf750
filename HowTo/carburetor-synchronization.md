# Carburetor synchronization

- Warm the engine to operating temperature.
- Connect vacuum gauges to all four intake ports.
- Adjust the sync screws between carb bodies until vacuum is balanced.
- Recheck idle speed after syncing.
- Reference video: [Syncing Carburetors on Honda VF750 - Revive 2 Ride](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EXc9i7zoA9g)

## If one cylinder is clearly lean

V4MuscleBike field example: a `1984 V30` owner rode about 120 km, then noticed a blue front-right pipe and a whitish plug. The bike still started easily, idled smoothly, and pulled well. That is the useful lesson: normal overall running does not rule out a local lean condition on one cylinder.

Practical order:

1. First confirm that cylinder synchronization is actually valid.
2. Check the suspect pipe temperature with an IR thermometer and compare it with the other cylinders.
3. If sync is valid, try final pilot-mixture adjustment with the idle-drop method.
4. Remember that the pilot screw only changes mixture within a small range.
5. If the lean condition does not respond to adjustment, do not keep winding the screw out; suspect partly plugged carb passages.

Signs that adjustment alone is not enough:

- blue or quickly bluing exhaust pipe;
- white or very light plug on one cylinder;
- rpm hanging after warm-up;
- pilot screw barely affects idle;
- one cylinder consistently hotter than the others.

At that point, clean/rebuild the carbs and verify the pilot circuits. A lean condition severe enough to blue the pipe can damage the engine or exhaust. See [how to clear a plugged pilot circuit](carburetor-cleaning.md#how-to-clear-a-plugged-pilot-circuit).

If the rack syncs but throttle response is still uneven, or one cylinder differs under load, check the [CV pistons, needles, and diaphragms](carburetor-cleaning.md#checking-cv-pistons-needles-and-diaphragms). A sticking piston or mis-seated diaphragm can look like incorrect mixture.
