# Common Carburetor Issues

This is the high-signal summary for the V45 Sabre carburetor work based on the manuals and notes already present in this repository.

## 1. "Runs Only On Choke" Usually Means The Pilot Circuit Is Still Dirty

The most common failure pattern on old VF750 carburetors is:

- stale fuel varnish in the pilot jets;
- blocked pilot air or fuel passages;
- partial blockage that survives a superficial spray-can cleaning.

Practical rule:
- if the bike only runs with choke or dies off-idle, assume the pilot circuit is still dirty until proven otherwise.

## 2. Synchronization Matters More Than People Want To Admit

These bikes can start and even idle badly while the carb rack is still far out of balance.

Typical signs:
- uneven idle;
- rough low-speed running;
- poor throttle pickup;
- one bank feeling lazier than the other.

Practical rule:
- after any rack removal, partial rebuild, cable disturbance, or idle adjustment work, plan to sync the carburetors again.

## 3. Intake Boots And Vacuum Leaks Are Carb Problems Too

The carburetors themselves are often blamed for symptoms created by:

- cracked intake insulators;
- loose boot clamps;
- aged vacuum caps and lines;
- poor sealing at the carb-to-boot interface.

Typical signs:
- hanging idle;
- lean surge;
- inconsistent idle speed;
- mixture settings that never quite respond correctly.

## 4. Diaphragms Cause Subtle But Serious Driveability Problems

A tiny pinhole or hardened diaphragm can create:

- flat spots;
- weak slide lift;
- bad midrange response;
- inconsistent cylinder response under load.

Practical rule:
- inspect every diaphragm closely and do not assume "good enough" rubber is actually good.

## 5. Float And Fuel Valve Problems Often Start Upstream

Flooding, wet plugs, or overflowing bowls are not always only a carb-body problem.

Check:
- float valve needles and seats;
- float height;
- contaminated fuel from the tank;
- the manual/automatic fuel valve behavior upstream;
- rust or debris reaching the rack again after cleaning.

## 6. Front/Rear Or Left/Right Carb Differences Matter

The manuals warn that some carb parts differ by carb position or bank.

Practical rule:
- do not mix jets, springs, needles, or link pieces casually;
- mark every carburetor by cylinder location before disassembly.

## 7. Year Scope Matters

This repo currently mixes:

- `1982 VF750S` factory service material;
- broader multi-year Haynes procedures;
- `1984-1985 VF700S` parts fiche pages.

Practical rule:
- use the 1982 factory shop-manual procedure first for the project bike;
- use the VF700S parts fiche mainly for component-family reference and comparison;
- verify any actual part number by year before ordering.
